Mr. Jang, the head of the New Focus International news agency and a former North Korean government official, said he received the information of the defection from someone who aided the workers’ escape. He said he expected the workers to arrive in South Korea in 10 to 15 days.

A spokesman for China’s Foreign Ministry said she had no information about the matter.

Another defection of a group of workers from North Korea’s network of some 130 restaurants in foreign countries would suggest continued weakness in Pyongyang’s control of the citizens it allows to work abroad.

Workers at restaurants are among some 50,000 North Koreans who human-rights groups estimate are based outside the country and forced to work long hours. Most of their income is sent back to the government in Pyongyang.
